https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s00134-020-05991-x
Short paper that gives us some base factors to consider relating to mortality.
These factors could be used to train a model for prognosis prediction of future victims

https://arxiv.org/pdf/2003.12150.pdf
The authors create a simple model that assumes a 100% connected population, giving a rather pessimistic estimate for infection.
The core Susceptible, Infected, Recovered (SIR) model they use is well defined and can be re-applied to other data for our analysis.
They add other factors and show how they incorporate them, so if we find other things we need to take into account, we can base it off this.

https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC7204193/
Very short overview of different applications of big data in terms of COVID-19 based data. Significant applications that we could
look at include indentification of infected cases, travel history, different symptoms, identification of the virus, analysis of virus movement,
lockdown affectiveness, people leaving or entering affected areas, increased speed of development of medical treatments and more...
